- Linux Distros
    - Linux distribution
        - operating system made from a software collection that is based upon the Linux kernel
        - available for a wide variety of systems ranging from
            - embedded devices (for example, OpenWrt) and
            - personal computers (for example, Ubuntu),
            - to powerful supercomputers (for example, Red Hat Enterprise Linux).
    - A typical Linux distribution comprises
        - Linux kernel,
        - GNU tools
        - libraries,
        - additional software,
        - documentation,
        - a window system (the most common being X11)
        - and a desktop environment (for example GNOME or KDE Plasma).
    - free and open-source software under the GNU General Public License,
    - applications,
        - desktop and laptop computers,
        - servers, and
        - embedded systems.
    - Some of the most popular Linux distributions include:
        - Ubuntu: A user-friendly distribution based on Debian, known for its ease of use and large community support.
        - Fedora: A cutting-edge distribution sponsored by Red Hat, known for its focus on innovation and the latest
          software.
        - Debian: A stable and versatile distribution that serves as the basis for many other distributions, including
          Ubuntu.
        - CentOS: A free and open-source distribution based on Red Hat Enterprise Linux, often used for servers and
          enterprise applications.
        - Arch Linux: A lightweight and flexible distribution that allows users to build their system from the ground
          up, known for its simplicity and customization options.
        - Linux Mint: A user-friendly distribution based on Ubuntu, known for its ease of use and attractive desktop
          environment.
        - openSUSE: A community-driven distribution that offers both a stable release (Leap) and a rolling release (
          Tumbleweed), known for its powerful configuration tools and support for multiple desktop environments.
        - Manjaro: A user-friendly distribution based on Arch Linux, known for its ease of use and pre-configured
          desktop environments.
        - Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL): A commercial distribution designed for enterprise use, known for its
          stability, security, and support options.
        - Kali Linux: A distribution based on Debian, designed for penetration testing and security auditing, known for
          its extensive collection of security tools.
        - Xfce: A lightweight desktop environment that can be used with various Linux distributions, known for its speed and
          low resource usage, making it suitable for older hardware.
